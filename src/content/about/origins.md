The list in the image illustrates a key turning point in my life: I was one of the 32 lucky ones selected for the coveted tuition-free National Fine Arts Academy back in my home country. This milestone marked the beginning of a lifelong focus on how what we present shapes the thoughts and emotions of others, getting me to think deeply about the problem-solving exercise of creating something that communicates its intended purpose.

Today, that foundational perspective sits at the core of how I engage with the world. I love to dissect everything from why we feel the way we do in certain spaces, to how difficult conversations can be designed to feel a bit lighter. Ultimately, it's all about experiences. I am incredibly lucky to spend my days refining them in the tight, sometimes messy, digital world.
